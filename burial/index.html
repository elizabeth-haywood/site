Due to limited space, the burial is only open to family. We welcome all extended family.

Please share this information with all family, but not more widely.

Bluestem is open to the public from dawn to dusk; all are welcome to visit her at any time after the burial.

What to Expect

  • Walking together to the gravesite through a wild and wonderful nature preserve
  • A one-hour service with poetry, remembrances, and music
  • Lowering her body into the grave as part of a green burial
  • You will be invited to place a flower (which we will provide) or a written note into the grave

Note: If you write a note ahead of time, please use compostable materials only (paper, cardboard, plant-based materials—no plastic or synthetic items). We will have compostable paper and pencils available on-site.

What to Wear

Dress warmly for near-freezing and gusty weather, with shoes that are comfortable for walking in the frosty meadow.

Parking

Park in the designated area before the cedar fence. Overflow parking is past the fence or along the tree line. Please carpool if possible, as parking is limited.

Essentials

Bring water and an umbrella if needed.

Accessibility

If you need assistance walking to the grave, let us know — we'll have a golf cart on hand to help.
